PJLink

What is PJLink?

PJLink is an open protocol standard designed for the remote control and monitoring of projectors over a network. Developed by the Japan Business Machine and Information System Industries Association (JBMIA), PJLink provides a unified method of communication between projectors and control systems, regardless of the manufacturer.

The primary goal of PJLink is interoperability. By adhering to this standard, devices from different brands (like Panasonic, NEC, Epson, Sony, etc.) can be controlled using the same set of commands. This greatly simplifies integration in environments with mixed hardware — such as schools, conference centers, auditoriums, or digital installations.

PJLink operates over TCP/IP, typically on port 4352, and supports a range of commands to control power, input sources, AV mute, lamp status, and error reporting. It also includes a basic authentication mechanism for security.

 

 

About projectors

A projector is an electronic device that receives video signals and displays images or videos by projecting them onto a surface, typically a screen or wall. Projectors are widely used in various settings — from home theaters and classrooms to conference rooms, museums, and large-scale installations.

There are several types of projectors, each using different imaging technologies:

  • LCD (Liquid Crystal Display): Offers good color reproduction and sharpness; commonly used in business and education.

  • DLP (Digital Light Processing): Known for high contrast ratios and smoother video; widely used in cinema and portable projectors.

  • LCoS (Liquid Crystal on Silicon): Combines advantages of LCD and DLP; used in high-end models for detailed image quality.

  • Laser & LED Projectors: Use solid-state light sources for longer lifespans, lower maintenance, and instant on/off capabilities.

Key features of modern projectors include:

  • Resolution (e.g., 1080p, 4K)

  • Brightness (measured in lumens)

  • Throw Ratio (distance-to-image size relationship)

  • Keystone Correction & Lens Shift (for image alignment)

  • Connectivity (HDMI, VGA, USB, LAN, Wi-Fi)

Many projectors today also support network control, allowing remote operation, monitoring, and automation. This is where protocols like PJLink come in — offering a standardized way to manage projectors across different brands from a central system.

In professional environments, projectors are often integrated into AV control systems for seamless presentations, signage, and immersive experiences.
